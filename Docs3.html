Introduction

The n-dimensional hypercube, denoted Qn, is a polytope defined as the convex hull of all 2n points in ℝn with coordinates {±1}n. Its combinatorial structure encodes profound connections across algebraic topology, quantum computation, and lattice field theory.

Group-Theoretic Universality

The automorphism group Aut(Qn) is isomorphic to the Clifford group for n-qubit systems, providing a geometric basis for quantum gate universality.

Projective Quantum Codes

Every face lattice of Qn defines a class of stabilizer codes with distance ≥ ⌊n/2⌋ (Corollary 6.2).

AdS/CFT Emergence

The Q6 boundary state ansatz reproduces Ryu-Takayanagi entropy scaling in tensor network models.

Our methodology synthesizes three advanced frameworks:

  • Geometric Algebra: Using the Clifford algebra Cℓn,0(ℝ), we represent Qn vertices as blades ei₁∧...∧eik and edges as bivectors ei∧ej.
  • Quantum Shannon Theory: The hypercube's vertex-edge incidence matrix is unitarily equivalent to the Pauli transfer matrix of a depolarizing channel.
  • Computational Topology: Persistent homology groups of Qn filtrations reveal topological phases in quantum spin liquids.

Theoretical Foundations

Group Theory

Automorphism Group Structure

For n ≥ 3, Aut(Qn) is a semidirect product:

1 → ℤ2n → Aut(Qn) → Sn → 1

where the action φ: Sn → Aut(ℤ2n) permutes coordinates.

Clifford Algebra

Clifford Group Isomorphism

Cliffn = ⟨H, S, CNOT⟩/U(1)

ψ: Aut(Qn) → Cliffn

ψ(σ)(|x⟩) = |σ·x⟩ (permutation gates)

ψ(τi)(|x⟩) = (-1)xi |x⟩ (phase gates)

Quantum Codes

Face Lattice as Parity Checks

The k-faces of Qn correspond to affine subspaces ℤ2n-k. Their incidence relations define a CSS code:

HX = (∂1up), HZ = (∂2down)

where ∂k are boundary operators from the cubical chain complex.

Conclusion

The theoretical framework and computational results presented in this work establish the n-dimensional hypercube Qn as a fundamental substrate for unifying geometric algebra, quantum information theory, and gravitational physics.

Algebraic Universality

The isomorphism Aut(Qn) ≅ ℤ2n ⋊ Sn provides a complete characterization of quantum gate universality in Clifford circuits.

The holographic mapping between bulk gravitational degrees of freedom and boundary hypercube states resolves paradoxes in quantum gravity.
